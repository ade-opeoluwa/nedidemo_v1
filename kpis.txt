NEDI Platform Analytics KPIs
----------------------------
1.	Exam Pass Rate vs % Qualified Teachers - Impact of teacher training
2.	Dropout Rate vs Teacher–Student Ratio - Overload impact 
3.	Rural-Urban Performance Gap vs Teacher Deployment Equity Index - Staffing inequality effects
4.	Gender Gap vs Female Teacher Ratio - More female teachers to higher female retention
5.	Average Exam Score by Teacher Qualification Level - Identifies whether higher teacher qualification levels translate into improved student performance
6.	Exam Pass Rate vs Teacher-to-Student Ratio - Identifies whether lower teaching loads are associated with improved student performance
7.	Exam Pass Rate vs Classroom Condition Index - Whether better classrooms improve results
8.	Average Class Performance vs Average Class Size - Shows optimal class size thresholds for effective teaching
9.	Pass Rate vs Electricity Availability - Role of electricity in learning environment
10.	Pass Rate vs ICT Access - Impact of digital resources in learning environment
11.	Dropout Rate vs Infrastructure Deficit Score - Whether poor facilities drive dropout
12.	Enrollment Growth vs Infrastructure Expansion - If building schools increases education access
13.	School Safety Facilities vs Attendance Stability - Shows whether secure schools sustain attendance during unrest or crises
14.	School Ranking vs Teacher Quality Index - High teacher quality → high ranking
15.	School Improvement vs Training Interventions
16.	School Results vs Inspection Frequency - More inspections → better compliance
17.	Data Reporting Timeliness vs Funding Allocation - Good data → better funding
18.	Payroll Timeliness vs Teacher Retention - Late pay → attrition (more public sector focused)
19.	Almajiri Enrollment vs Literacy Rates - Higher inclusion → higher literacy
20.	Special Needs Enrollment vs Facility Access - Better access → higher enrollment
21.	Support Coverage vs Enrollment Growth - Highlights whether increases in enrollment are matched by support e.g. grants, funding, improved infrastructure, indicating potential gaps in resources or efficiency in resource allocation
22.	Almajiri Enrollment vs State OOSC Rate - Correlation shows states with low enrolment → high OOSC 
23.	Scholarship Coverage vs Retention - Scholarships → lower dropout
24.	Gender-Adjusted OOSC Risk Index - Pinpoints states where girls face disproportionate exclusion
25.	Almajiri and OOSC Age Transition Risk - Identifies critical ages where intervention should intensify
26.	Scholarship Coverage vs Academic Performance - Average Exam Score of Beneficiaries vs Non-beneficiaries
27.	OOSC Reintegration Efficiency - Shows which demographics are hardest to bring back into school
28.	OOSC Learning Catch-Up Index - Reveals how late entry affects learning recovery
29.	Multi-Vulnerability Exposure Index - Identifies children facing compounded risks, not single-factor issues
30.	Vulnerability-to-Promotion Ratio - Identifies whether vulnerable students are being left behind in progression, not just enrollment
31.	Enrollment-to-Completion Conversion Rate - Shows how efficiently the system converts access into successful outcomes, not just enrollment growth
32.	Enrollment Growth vs Teacher Growth Gap - Early warning for future overcrowding and declining quality
33.	Gender Survival Rate Gap - Highlights age ranges with higher dropout risk for girls compared to boys
34.	Gender Learning Efficiency Index - Reveals whether access converts into achievement equally across genders
35.	Adolescent Girls Attrition Curve by State - Highlights puberty-linked dropout spikes by location
36.	Transition Shock Index - Identifies system leakage points during level transitions (primary to secondary, secondary to tertiary)
37.	Age-Vulnerability Dropout Risk - Pinpoints ages where compounded vulnerabilities most threaten retention, guiding age-targeted interventions
38.	Female OOSC Re-Enrollment Efficiency - Measures effectiveness of programs targeting girls specifically, highlighting gender-specific program gaps
39.	Regional Equity Gap Index - Identifies regional disparities in performance, directing targeted interventions and resources
